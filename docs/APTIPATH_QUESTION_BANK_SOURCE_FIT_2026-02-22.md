# AptiPath Question Bank Source Fit (India) - 2026-02-22

## Objective
Map the referenced global + India sources into an India-ready AptiPath question-bank strategy for:
1. Parent concerns
2. Student readiness
3. Career cluster direction

## Source Fit Summary
These sources are excellent for frameworks, pathway data, and labor-market signals.
They are **not direct exam-style question banks** for school/competitive prep.

| Source | Best use in AptiPath | India fit | Question-bank role |
|---|---|---|---|
| OECD teenage career preparation | Career uncertainty, exposure design | High | Create scenario items (career awareness, decision maturity) |
| WEF Future of Jobs 2025 | Future skills and role shifts | High | Update AI-era readiness and transferable skills items |
| OECD AI and skills | Task-shift and skill-demand evidence | High | Build AI fluency, verification, and ethics items |
| Stanford Life Design | Exploration mindset and prototyping | Medium-High | Add design-thinking reflection prompts |
| Australian Blueprint | Structured development competencies | Medium-High | Build age-stage progression rubrics |
| O*NET tools | Occupation/skill architecture | High | Skill-to-career mapping for recommendation engine |
| NIRF + Ranking Framework | Quality-filter for higher-ed options | High | Post-assessment pathway filtering, not test items |
| NAAC ecosystem references | Accreditation signal | High | Trust filters in pathway recommendations |
| NCVET NQR | NSQF qualification pathways | Very High | Vocational/applied alternatives in recommendation graph |
| Apprenticeship India / NAPS-NATS | Apprenticeship routes | Very High | Work-integrated fallback pathway options |

## Parent vs Student Focus
Parent-focused item banks:
1. Budget feasibility
2. Geography mobility
3. Preferred language
4. Coaching tolerance
5. Preferred education model
6. Risk and fallback expectations
7. Parent-student alignment readiness

Student-focused item banks:
1. Cognitive aptitude
2. Applied problem solving
3. Interest/work preference
4. Values/motivation
5. Learning behavior
6. AI-era readiness
7. Career reality tolerance (uncertainty, effort, communication demand)

## Career Clusters to Prioritize for India
1. Engineering and Emerging Tech
2. Health and Life Sciences
3. Commerce, Business and Analytics
4. Design, Media and Creative Tech
5. Public Policy and Social Impact
6. Skilled Trades and Vocational (NSQF/NQR linked)
7. Apprenticeship-led applied careers (NAPS/NATS linked)

## Question Bank Update Model (Next Phase)
1. Monthly source scan:
   - OECD/WEF/O*NET/NCVET/NIRF changes
2. Draft item generation:
   - New/updated scenarios and rubrics tagged by section and cluster
3. SME review gate:
   - Psychometric + curriculum + language review
4. Version publish:
   - `v2.x -> v3.x` with changelog
5. Post-publish analytics:
   - difficulty, discrimination, time, confidence, skip rate
6. Retirement policy:
   - stale/biased items moved to `RETIRED`

## Implementation Note
For exam-prep style content (NEET/JEE/CUET/CAT), source from:
1. Official PYQ sets
2. NCERT/board curriculum-aligned banks
3. Licensed publisher datasets
4. Internal SME-authored modeled papers

Global policy/framework reports should shape competencies and pathways, not replace subject question banks.
