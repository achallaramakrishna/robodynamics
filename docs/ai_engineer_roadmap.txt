Example for "AI Engineer" (Inclined towards AI/Tech Cluster)
Career Overview
AI Engineers design and build intelligent systems like chatbots, recommendation engines, and autonomous vehicles. By 2036, India will need 1.4M+ AI roles due to digital transformation (source: NASSCOM). Starting salary: ₹12–25LPA.
General Roadmap

Foundation (Grades 8–10): Build basics in math, science, and coding. Focus on logical thinking.
Preparation (Grades 11–12): Choose PCM + Computer Science stream. Prepare for entrance exams.
Higher Education (Post-12): Pursue B.Tech in AI/ML or CSE from top institutes.
Skill Building & Experience: Learn Python, ML tools; do internships/projects.
Career Launch (2034–2036): Entry-level roles at tech firms; upskill in advanced AI.

Example for "AI Engineer" (Student in Grade 9, CBSE Board, High Tech Interest Score)
Personalized Overview
Based on your test, you're 4.2/5 inclined to AI/Tech! As a grade 9 CBSE student, this path aligns with your logical thinking and curiosity. By 2036, AI roles will boom in India (1.4M+ demand). Expected salary: ₹12–30LPA starting.
Grade-Specific Roadmap (Starting from Grade 9)
1. What to Study

Grade 9: Focus on Math (Algebra/Geometry), Science (Physics basics), English (communication). Introduce Computer Science if available.
Grade 10: Strengthen PCM; aim for 90%+ in boards. Take intro CS courses.
Grades 11–12: PCM + Computer Science stream. Cover calculus, probability, programming.
Post-12: B.Tech AI/ML or CSE (4 years).
2034–2036: Optional M.Tech or certs (e.g., Google AI).

2. Skills to Gather

Grade 9: Basic coding (Scratch/Python basics), logical puzzles.
Grade 10: Python (loops/functions), data basics (Excel).
Grades 11–12: Algorithms, ML intro (scikit-learn), Git/version control.
Post-12: Advanced ML (TensorFlow), cloud (AWS free tier), ethics.
By 2036: Specialize in NLP/CV; soft skills like teamwork.

3. What Kind of Projects to Do

Grade 9: Simple chatbot in Python; puzzle solver app.
Grade 10: Weather predictor using data; basic ML model (e.g., Iris classification on Kaggle).
Grades 11–12: Image recognizer; participate in hackathons (HackIndia).
Post-12: Open-source contribution; AI project for social good (e.g., crop disease detector).
By 2036: Portfolio of 5+ projects; internship projects.

4. Where to Study

Grades 8–12: Top CBSE schools with CS labs (e.g., DPS Bengaluru).
Entrance Exams: JEE Main/Advanced (for IITs), BITSAT, VITEEE.
Colleges: IIT Bombay/Delhi (CSE/AI), IIIT Hyderabad, BITS Pilani, NIT Trichy, VIT Vellore.
Online Resources: Coursera (Google AI), Khan Academy (math), freeCodeCamp (coding).
By 2036: Specialize at IISc or abroad (e.g., Stanford AI certs).

90-Day Action Plan (Personalized)

Days 1–30: Daily 30 min Python practice; read “AI for Beginners” book.
Days 31–60: Build first project (chatbot); join online AI club.
Days 61–90: Mock JEE practice; mentor session (find on LinkedIn).

Long-Term Milestones to 2036

2030: College graduation.
2032: First internship (TCS/Infosys AI lab).
2036: Full-time AI Engineer role.
