# AptiPath360 Objection Handling Guide

## Objection 1: "We already have counseling in school."

Response:
"That is great. AptiPath360 strengthens your counseling with structured evidence and a standardized student report. It supports your counselors, not replaces them."

## Objection 2: "Students are already busy."

Response:
"The flow is designed to be focused and practical. The output saves future time by reducing confusion and repeated trial-and-error decisions."

## Objection 3: "Is this just another aptitude test?"

Response:
"No. AptiPath360 combines aptitude with motivation, behavior, interest, and career reality checks, then gives a 90-day action plan."

## Objection 4: "Parents may not cooperate."

Response:
"Parent input is streamlined. Even a brief parent context significantly improves recommendation quality and alignment."

## Objection 5: "How do we trust the recommendations?"

Response:
"Recommendations are based on multi-section signals and are presented with rationale. The report is designed for discussion and action, not blind acceptance."

## Objection 6: "What is the ROI for us?"

Response:
"Institutions use AptiPath360 to improve counseling quality, parent trust, and student decision confidence. It creates a measurable guidance process, not ad-hoc advice."

## Objection 7: "Can we start small?"

Response:
"Yes. We recommend a pilot batch first, then scale based on completion and feedback."

## Objection 8: "What if a student wants to change stream later?"

Response:
"That is exactly why this is useful early. AptiPath360 helps detect mismatch and supports corrective decisions before high-cost mistakes."
