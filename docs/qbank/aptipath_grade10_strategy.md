# AptiPath360 — Grade 10 Complete Assessment Strategy
## "Grade 10 is the fork in the road. One wrong stream choice echoes for a decade."

---

## 1. WHY GRADE 10 IS THE MOST PIVOTAL YEAR

Grade 10 (age 15-16) is the **single most consequential year** in an Indian student's academic life.

- They are **0 years** from the PCM/PCB/Commerce/Humanities stream decision — it happens NOW
- Board exams create the first high-stakes academic pressure — responses reflect coached vs authentic thinking
- Stream choice at this age **locks 80% of career pathways** for the next 6-8 years (Grade 11 + entrance exams + degree)
- A student who chooses PCM when they should have chosen Commerce loses 2-4 years before course-correcting
- Parental pressure peaks at Grade 10 — data-backed stream recommendations **prevent family-driven misalignment**
- Students at this age have enough cognitive maturity for formal reasoning, ethical judgment, and self-assessment
- India-specific: JEE/NEET/CLAT/CA preparation begins in Grade 11 — the stream decision determines WHICH exam pipeline they enter

**Grade 10 Design Principles:**
1. Board-exam-grade difficulty — questions must feel intellectually legitimate to a student already preparing for boards
2. Stream detection is the PRIMARY objective — every section contributes signal toward PCM/PCB/Commerce/Humanities fit
3. NEW: BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION section separates PCM vs PCB within Science stream
4. Career Reality questions test awareness of actual pathways (JEE, NEET, CA, CLAT, KVPY) — not hypotheticals
5. AI Readiness is expanded to 6 questions — AI literacy is now a career differentiator, not a bonus
6. Applied Challenge scenarios are multi-stakeholder, trade-off-heavy, and reflect real-world complexity
7. Critical thinking questions (correlation vs causation, data sufficiency) test reasoning maturity
8. 70 questions total (up from 60 in Grade 8) — the extra 10 come from expanded sections and the new BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION
9. Time pressure is acknowledged but managed — board-exam students are accustomed to timed assessments
10. Parent engagement strategy centers entirely on stream choice — this is the decision they are paying for

**Key structural differences from Grade 8:**
- Total questions: 70 (vs 60) — +10 questions across expanded and new sections
- CORE_APTITUDE: 12 questions (vs 10) — includes board-level quadratics, trigonometry, probability, critical reasoning
- APPLIED_CHALLENGE: 10 questions (vs 8) — multi-stakeholder scenarios with ethical trade-offs
- AI_READINESS: 6 questions (vs 4) — AI is now a career-defining competency
- CAREER_REALITY: 6 questions (vs 4) — must test actual JEE/NEET/CA/CLAT/KVPY pathway knowledge
- BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION: 4 questions (vs 0) — **entirely new section** critical for PCM vs PCB determination
- GENERAL_AWARENESS: 4 questions (vs 6) — trimmed, sharper focus on policy + science awareness
- REASONING_IQ: 2 questions (vs 4) — trimmed, but increased in difficulty (3D spatial, formal logic)
- STEM_FOUNDATION: 6 questions (vs 4) — expanded to cover Physics, Chemistry, and Math at board-exam level

**Non-MCQ questions = 30 out of 70 questions (43%)** — maintains the "not an exam" feel while accommodating harder content.

## 3. QUESTION TYPE TAXONOMY (10 Types Used)

| Type | Code | Used In | Scoring | Grade 10 Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Multiple Choice | MCQ | CORE_APTITUDE, AI, STEM, BIO, GA, CR, RIQ | Correct/Wrong -> 0 or weightage | "If x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0, the values of x are:" |
| Fill in the Blank | FILL_BLANK | CORE_APTITUDE, STEM | Exact match -> 0 or weightage | "Rs.10000 at 10% CI for 2 years = Rs.___" |
| True / False | TRUE_FALSE | BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION | Correct/Wrong -> 0 or 3 | "Antibiotics are effective against viral infections" |
| Situational Judgment | SITUATIONAL | APPLIED_CHALLENGE, VALUES, CAREER, AI, LEARNING | Signal mapping per option | "AI factory vs forest: your recommendation to the village council:" |
| Likert Scale | LIKERT | INTEREST, VALUES, LEARNING | 1-5 maps to cluster + stream signal | "I am fascinated by how economies work — inflation, GDP, supply & demand (1-5)" |
| Rank Order | RANK_ORDER | INTEREST, VALUES | Item-to-stream mapping | "Rank: PCM subjects / PCB subjects / Commerce subjects / Humanities subjects" |
| Card Sort | CARD_SORT | INTEREST | Career/Hobby/No bucket -> stream weight | "Sort: Surgery, AI products, Business empire, Legal cases, Clean energy" |
| Multi-Select | MULTI_SELECT | LEARNING_BEHAVIOR | Each selection = independent signal | "Which board prep strategies work for you? Select all" |
| Story Insight | STORY_INSIGHT | Mandatory (separate) | Narrative -> AI interpretation | Open-ended paragraph: "What do you want to build, discover, or change?" |
| Career Intent | CAREER_INTENT | End of test | Direct selection | "Pick up to 5 careers you are seriously considering" |

**Grade 10 Type Distribution:**
- Pure aptitude (MCQ/FILL_BLANK with correct answers): 34 questions (49%)
- Behavioral/Preference (LIKERT, RANK_ORDER, CARD_SORT, MULTI_SELECT): 22 questions (31%)
- Situational Judgment (no single "correct" answer — signal mapping): 14 questions (20%)

This distribution reflects Grade 10's dual purpose: **measure ability AND detect stream preference**.

## 5. STREAM DETECTION ALGORITHM

### 5.1 — Why Stream Detection is the Primary Output for Grade 10

Unlike Grade 8 (where career cluster detection is exploratory), Grade 10's output must produce a **concrete, defensible stream recommendation**. Parents are paying for this specific decision.

The four streams and their mappings:

| Stream | Code | Career Clusters Served | Key Exams | Required Signals |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PCM** (Physics-Chemistry-Math) | PCM | TECH, ENGINEERING, GREEN, some BUSINESS | JEE Main, JEE Advanced, BITSAT, VITEEE | High CORE_APTITUDE math + STEM_FOUNDATION physics/chem + TECH/ENGINEERING interest |
| **PCB** (Physics-Chemistry-Biology) | PCB | HEALTH, RESEARCH, some GREEN | NEET-UG, KVPY, IISER entrance | High BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION + STEM_FOUNDATION + HEALTH interest + empathy signal |
| **Commerce** | COMM | BUSINESS, some CREATIVE, some LAW | CA Foundation, CLAT, CAT (later) | Economics LIKERT 4-5 + finance aptitude + wealth-building VALUES + Commerce subject preference |
| **Humanities** | HUM | SOCIAL, LAW, CREATIVE, some BUSINESS | CLAT, CUET, UPSC (later) | Social consciousness + verbal-creative + debate interest + justice VALUES |

### 5.2 — Stream Score Computation

Each student gets a score for all four streams. The stream with the highest score becomes the primary recommendation.

```
stream_score[PCM] = (
    0.25 × CORE_APTITUDE_math_subscore +
    0.20 × STEM_FOUNDATION_physics_chem_subscore +
    0.20 × INTEREST_WORK_pcm_signal +
    0.10 × REASONING_IQ_spatial_score +
    0.10 × AI_READINESS_score +
    0.10 × VALUES_tech_engineering_signal +
    0.05 × CAREER_REALITY_jee_awareness
)

stream_score[PCB] = (
    0.25 × BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION_score +
    0.20 × STEM_FOUNDATION_physics_chem_subscore +
    0.20 × INTEREST_WORK_pcb_signal +
    0.15 × VALUES_health_impact_signal +
    0.10 × CAREER_REALITY_neet_awareness +
    0.10 × LEARNING_BEHAVIOR_depth_persistence
)

stream_score[COMMERCE] = (
    0.25 × INTEREST_WORK_economics_signal +
    0.20 × CORE_APTITUDE_finance_subscore +
    0.20 × VALUES_wealth_business_signal +
    0.15 × CAREER_REALITY_commerce_awareness +
    0.10 × APPLIED_CHALLENGE_business_signal +
    0.10 × LEARNING_BEHAVIOR_organized_strategic
)

stream_score[HUMANITIES] = (
    0.25 × INTEREST_WORK_humanities_signal +
    0.20 × VALUES_social_justice_signal +
    0.20 × INTEREST_WORK_verbal_creative_signal +
    0.15 × APPLIED_CHALLENGE_social_legal_signal +
    0.10 × GENERAL_AWARENESS_score +
    0.10 × REASONING_IQ_logical_score
)
```

### 5.3 — Stream Decision Rules

```
Rule 1: CLEAR WINNER
  If max(stream_scores) - second_highest > 0.15:
    → Recommend the winning stream with HIGH confidence
    → "Strong PCM fit detected"

Rule 2: CLOSE CALL (Science vs Commerce or Science vs Humanities)
  If max(stream_scores) - second_highest ≤ 0.15 AND streams are different categories:
    → Recommend both with reasoning
    → "You show strong aptitude for both PCM and Commerce. Here's what each path looks like..."
    → Trigger Phase 3 disambiguation questions

Rule 3: PCM vs PCB DIFFERENTIATOR
  If both PCM and PCB are top 2 with gap ≤ 0.10:
    → Use BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION score as the tiebreaker
    → If BIO_FOUNDATION score ≥ 75%: lean PCB
    → If BIO_FOUNDATION score < 50%: lean PCM
    → If 50-75%: recommend "PCM with Biology as optional" or present both paths

Rule 4: CONFLICTING SIGNALS
  If interest signals conflict with aptitude signals:
    → Flag: "Your interests point to [X] but your aptitude is strongest in [Y]"
    → Provide both paths with honest trade-off analysis
    → Trigger wellbeing check: "Are you being pressured toward a specific stream?"

Rule 5: UNDECIDED
  If no stream scores above 0.50:
    → Do NOT force a recommendation
    → Recommend: "Explore all streams for 3 more months. Here's what to try for each..."
    → Schedule re-assessment
```

## 7. ACCURACY VERIFICATION SYSTEM — HOW TO ACHIEVE 100%

### 7.1 — Pre-Launch Quality Gates (8 gates, ALL must pass)

| Gate | What It Checks | Pass Criteria | Tool |
|---|---|---|---|
| **G1 — Blueprint** | Every question maps to section + bloom level + cluster + stream signal | 100% coverage, no orphan questions, every question has stream_signal | DB constraint check |
| **G2 — Language** | Reading level appropriate for Grade 10 board-exam students | Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level ≤ 10.5 | Automated lint |
| **G3 — Distractor** | MCQ wrong options are plausible but clearly wrong | SME rubric: each distractor must represent a real misconception at Grade 10 level | Manual review |
| **G4 — Bias** | No gender/caste/religion/region/board bias in language or scenarios | Zero bias flags on approved lexicon scan + CBSE/ICSE/State board neutrality | Semi-automated |
| **G5 — Duplicate** | No two questions test the same construct at the same bloom level | Semantic similarity < 0.82 between any pair within the same section | Cosine similarity scan |

### Cluster Score Formula (Grade 10 — stream-weighted):

```
cluster_score = (
  Σ(MCQ/FILL_BLANK correct × career_weight × bloom_weight × stream_alignment) +
  Σ(LIKERT score × signal_weight × stream_alignment) +
  Σ(RANK_ORDER position × positional_weight) +
  Σ(SITUATIONAL option × option_cluster_weight) +
  Σ(CARD_SORT bucket × bucket_weight) +
  BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION_bonus (for HEALTH/PCB clusters) +
  AI_READINESS_bonus (for TECH clusters)
) / total_possible_score
```

Where:
- `career_weight`: each question's declared career_cluster_map signal weight (0.1 to 1.0)
- `bloom_weight`: ANALYZE/EVALUATE questions get 1.5x weight; APPLY gets 1.2x; REMEMBER/UNDERSTAND get 1.0x
- `stream_alignment`: 1.2x bonus if the question's stream signal matches the cluster's required stream
- `positional_weight` for RANK_ORDER: Rank 1 = 5pts, Rank 2 = 4pts, Rank 3 = 3pts, Rank 4 = 2pts, Rank 5 = 1pt
- `bucket_weight` for CARD_SORT: "Career" = 3pts, "Hobby" = 1pt, "Not interested" = 0pt
- `BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION_bonus`: +0.15 to HEALTH cluster score if BIO_FOUNDATION ≥ 75%
- `AI_READINESS_bonus`: +0.10 to TECH cluster score if AI_READINESS ≥ 80%

### Stream Fit Score (unique to Grade 10):

```
stream_fit_score = (
  0.40 × cluster_score_for_stream_clusters +
  0.25 × direct_stream_preference_signals +
  0.20 × aptitude_match_score +
  0.15 × career_reality_awareness_score
)
```

The stream fit score is the TOP-LEVEL output. Career cluster scores are secondary — they refine WHICH career within the chosen stream, not which stream itself.

## 14. BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION — DESIGN RATIONALE (NEW SECTION)

### Why Biology Gets Its Own Section at Grade 10

In Grades 8 and 9, Biology questions lived inside STEM_FOUNDATION. At Grade 10, Biology becomes a separate section for one critical reason: **it is the single most important differentiator between PCM and PCB**.

A student who scores high on Physics + Math but low on Biology should choose PCM.
A student who scores high on Biology + Chemistry but moderate on Math should consider PCB.
A student who scores high on everything needs the INTEREST_WORK section to differentiate.

Without a dedicated BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION section, the system cannot reliably distinguish PCM from PCB students.

### BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION Question Design:

| Q# | Topic | Bloom Level | What It Tests | PCB Signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| BF_01 | Mitosis vs Meiosis | UNDERSTAND | Cell division knowledge — fundamental to all biology | Can the student distinguish basic cell biology concepts? |
| BF_02 | Genetics / DNA | UNDERSTAND | Heredity and molecular biology basics | Does the student understand the genetic code — gateway to biotech and medicine? |
| BF_03 | Anaemia Diagnosis | APPLY | Clinical reasoning from a blood report | Can the student apply biology to a real medical scenario? (strongest HEALTH signal) |
| BF_04 | Antibiotics vs Viruses | UNDERSTAND | Health literacy and microbiology basics | Does the student have foundational health science knowledge? |

### Scoring Rules for BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION:

```
BIO_SCORE = (correct_answers / 4) × 100

If BIO_SCORE ≥ 75% (3 or 4 correct):
  → Strong PCB signal
  → Add +0.15 to PCB stream score
  → Add +0.15 to HEALTH cluster score
  → Flag: "Biology aptitude supports medical/biotech/research pathways"

If BIO_SCORE = 50% (2 correct):
  → Neutral — Biology is neither a strength nor a weakness
  → No stream score adjustment
  → Flag: "Biology aptitude is average — stream decision should rely on interest signals"

If BIO_SCORE ≤ 25% (0 or 1 correct):
  → Weak PCB signal
  → Add -0.10 to PCB stream score (penalty)
  → Flag: "Biology aptitude is below expected level for PCB/medical pathways"
  → If student's INTEREST signals point to PCB despite low BIO score:
    → Conflict flag: "Interest in medicine detected but biology fundamentals need strengthening"
    → Recommend: "If you choose PCB, invest heavily in biology coaching from Day 1 of Grade 11"
```

### BIOLOGY_FOUNDATION in the Adaptive Flow:

- Phase 1 includes 2 BIO questions (BF_01, BF_02) — early PCM/PCB signal
- Phase 2 includes remaining 2 BIO questions (BF_03, BF_04)
- If Phase 1 BIO score = 0/2 AND student's intake says "leaning PCB" -> AI Call #1 flags a potential conflict
- If Phase 1 BIO score = 2/2 AND STEM Physics score is also high -> AI Call #1 flags "strong all-rounder, needs interest-based differentiation"
- Phase 3 uses BIO score as a key input for deep-dive routing: high BIO -> PCB deep dive, low BIO -> PCM deep dive
